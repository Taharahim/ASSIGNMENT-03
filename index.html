Exploring the World of Literature

Classic novels have profoundly influenced literature by addressing timeless themes and offering deep insights into human nature and society.
Jane Austen's Pride and Prejudice explores themes of love, social class, and personal growth , setting a precedent for the modern romance genre.
George Orwell's 1984 delves into the dangers of totalitarianism and the loss of individual freedom, serving as a powerful political allegory.
Harper Lee's To Kill a Mockingbirdconfronts themes of racial injustice and moral courage, inspiring readers to reflect on ethics and equality.
Herman Melville's Moby-Dickexamines obsession, revenge, and man's struggle against nature, solidifying its place as a literary masterpiece of philosophical depth.
Lastly, F. Scott Fitzgerald's The Great Gatsbycritiques the illusion of the American Dream and societal decadence , capturing the spirit and disillusionment of the Jazz.
These novels continue to shape literary traditions and challenge readers to engage with complex social and personal issues
